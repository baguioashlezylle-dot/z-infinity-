LATEST MISSION UPDATES & DISCOVERIES

ARTEMIS II: PREPARING FOR LUNAR ASCENT

NASA is currently in the final stages of the "Wet Dress Rehearsal" for the Artemis II mission. This mission marks the first time in over 50 years that humans will journey to the Moon's vicinity. The crew is set to perform a lunar flyby, testing the Orion spacecraft's life-support systems in deep space.

The four-person crew: Wiseman, Glover, Koch, and Hansen.

JWST DETECTS WATER VAPOR ON EXOPLANET LHS 475 b

The James Webb Space Telescope has provided atmospheric data for LHS 475 b, an Earth-sized rocky planet. While the planet is significantly hotter than Earth, the detection of atmospheric components marks a major milestone in our ability to characterize worlds that could potentially host life.

EUROPA CLIPPER: THE SEARCH FOR AN OCEAN WORLD

Now hurtling through the outer solar system, the Europa Clipper mission is designed to investigate Jupiter's moon, Europa. Scientists believe a vast saltwater ocean exists beneath its icy crust, containing more water than all of Earth's oceans combined.

PERSEVERANCE CAPTURES NEW SAMPLES IN NERETVA VALLIS

The Perseverance rover has successfully collected its 24th core sample from an ancient riverbed. These samples are crucial for the future Mars Sample Return mission, as they contain minerals that only form in the presence of liquid water.

FAST RADIO BURSTS FROM DEEP SPACE

Astronomers have traced a series of repeating Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) to a magnetar in a distant galaxy. These intense pulses of radio waves last only milliseconds but release as much energy as the Sun does in a year, challenging our understanding of high-energy physics.
