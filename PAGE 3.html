Beyond Our Galaxy

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy is a barred spiral galaxy and is the nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way. Cataloged as Messier 31, it features a diameter of approximately 152,000 light-years and is situated 2.5 million light-years from Earth.

Pillars of Creation

These towers of cosmic dust and gas stretch 4 to 5 light-years tall within the Eagle Nebula. Located 7,000 light-years away, they represent a small but iconic region where new stars are actively forming.

Crab Nebula

This expanding remnant resulted from a supernova explosion recorded by astronomers in 1054 AD. The relic has since grown to a width of approximately 11 light-years.
