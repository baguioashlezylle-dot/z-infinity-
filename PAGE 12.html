The Planet of Life

A Unique World

While Earth is only the fifth largest planet in the solar system, it is the only world in our solar system with liquid water on the surface. Just slightly larger than nearby Venus, Earth is the biggest of the four planets closest to the Sun, all of which are made of rock and metal.

Earth is not just a planet; it is a symbol of life and renewed purpose, providing the perfect conditions for a vast variety of ecosystems to flourish.

Atmosphere and Water

Earth's atmosphere is a protective shield composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and 1% other gases. This unique composition allows life to thrive and maintains an average surface temperature of approximately 15°C.

About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered with water, earning it the nickname "The Blue Planet".

The Deep Ocean (The Hadal Zone)

Over 80% of the ocean remains unmapped and unexplored. Recent expeditions to the Hadal Zone (depths below 6,000 meters) have discovered bizarre life forms that survive without sunlight and under immense pressure.

Recent Discovery: In 2023, scientists discovered over 100 new species, including deep-sea corals and sea cucumbers, off the coast of Chile.

Subglacial Lakes (Antarctica)

Hidden beneath miles of Antarctic ice are hundreds of freshwater lakes that have been isolated from the rest of the world for millions of years. These "lost worlds" may hold ancient microorganisms and clues to how life might exist on icy moons like Europa.

The "Hidden" World of Caves (Son Doong)

Some of the largest environments on Earth were hidden until very recently. For example, Son Doong in Vietnam, the world’s largest cave, was only fully explored in 2009. It is so large it has its own localized weather system, clouds, and a jungle inside.

Recent Discovery: Explorers recently found a hidden underwater tunnel connecting Son Doong to another cave, making the system even more massive than previously thought..
